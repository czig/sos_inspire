SS Torrey Canyon Oil Spill


Caleb Ziegler

Background

  • Oil tanker ran aground on reef off southwest coast of UK
  • Occured March 18, 1967
  • Worst oil spill at the time
  • Spilled 25-36 million gallons of crude oil

Setting

  • Origin: Kuwait
  • Destination: Milford Haven, Wales
  • Wreck occured near Scilly Isles on Seven Stones Reef off southwest coast of UK

Course of Events

  • 0230: Capt Rugiati retires for evening
  • 0600: First Officer wakes Capt Rugiati with status update
  • 0630: First Officer spots Scilly Isles
  • 0700: Capt Rugiati arrives on bridge
  • 0800: First Officer relieved, new watch takes over
  • 0838: Capt Rugiati realizes that new crew made mistake in calculating position
  • 0850: SS Torrey Canyon runs aground in Seven Stones Reef
    • 0600: Capt told first officer to wake him with status update at 0600 if Scilly Isles are not seen by then
    • 0630: First officer sees ship is off course by 8 miles (thought Isles would be to right, but they appeared to left). Corrects course. Notifies Capt Rugiati, who admonishes him and tells him to change course back to it was. Capt gives First Officer his intent for passing Isles here (intends to go between Isles and Seven Stones Reef).
    • 0800: new watch is very inexperienced (maiden voyage for both members) and doesn't know captain's intent
    • 0818: Capt Rugiati changes course to move to port of Seven Stones Reef, but fails to compensate for wind and ocean current
    • 0830: SS Torrey Canyon runs into submerged fishing nets; presence of more fishing nets prevents him from turning harder to port
    • 0840: Fishing vessels to port of SS Torrey Canyon, while no fishing vessels and deep water to starboard, but Capt Rugiati turns to port. When they finally get a good reading and realize the danger at hand, confusion about steering controls in last 10 minutes compounded the issue. Last manuever is a hard turn to port

The Person

  • Poor communicatior
  • Impulsive
  • Lack of trust in subordinates
  • Demanding
  • Tempermental
  • Rigid
  • Sleep deprived
  • Captains intent was not communicated prior to execution
  • No planning, captain decided on plan during execution
  • Did not trust first officer to make a decision
  • Micro-managed rather than delegating; told first officer to wake him when Scilly Isles spotted, but not clear why he wanted to be woken; actively in charge of steering ship
  • Admonished first officer and told him to change plans when first officer took initiative (lack of emotional intelligence, self-awareness, and awareness of his impact on crew)
  • Stuck to plan when plan started to fall apart

Situation

  • Ship's position
  • Ocean current
  • Wind
  • Steering system
  • Three other vessels
  • Fishing Nets
  • Took way too long to get accurate position
  • Did not know about ocean current or wind, both of which were pushing the ship east
  • Steering system unnecesarilly complex
  • Other vessels and fishing nets hampered maneuverability; failed to adjust plan based on vessels and nets

Connectivity

  • Leading Down
    • Captain's intent not known
    • Did not empower subordinates
    • Ineffective team
  • Leading Beyond
    • No communication with other vessels
  • Delegation not possible without captain's intent being known
  • Subordinates were not empowered to take initiative due to captain's micromanagement and temper
  • Inexperienced members in team lead to ineffective team
  • Fishing vessels and Seven Stones Lightship had different perspective that could have helped SS Torrey Canyon navigate channel, but failed to reach out to them

Parting Thoughts

  • Root cause: poor leadership
  • If plan generated in advance with thoughtful contingencies, stick to plan
  • If plan generated on short notice, be willing to adjust plan
    • Slowing down could have prevented oil spill
    • Google maps example
